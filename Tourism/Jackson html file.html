Jackson: LeFleur's Bluff

The city sits on the Pearl River and is located in the greater Jackson Prairie region of Mississippi. The settlement on the Pearl River that gave birth to Jackson was first called LeFleur's Bluff, named for French-Canadian trader Louis LeFleur, who had founded a trading post on the site

Mississippi Museum of Natural Science teaches visitors about the natural history of Mississippi through educational exhibits, specimen collections, and interactive habitats. Among the areas devoted to wildlife is a 100,000-gallon aquarium network, which houses more than 200 native species of aquatic life.

The Jackson Zoo is a hit with families and animal-lovers, home to more than 200 species of animals from five continents, including endangered species. African animals include primates like chimpanzees and colobus monkeys, birds like storks and ostriches, and even an African fat-tailed gecko.
